Abdul Sattar Edhi

He was a Pakistani philanthropist, ascetic, and humanitarian who founded the Edhi Foundation, which runs the world's largest volunteer ambulance network, along with homeless shelters, rehab centres, and orphanages across Pakistan.

Born: February 28, 1928, Bantva, India Died: July 8, 2016, Karachi, Pakistan Spouse: Bilquis Edhi (m. 1966–2016) Awards: Lenin Peace Prize

The Edhi Foundation

The Edhi Foundation (Urdu: ایدھی فاؤنڈیشن‎) is a non-profit social welfare program in Pakistan, founded by Abdul Sattar Edhi in 1951. Edhi until his death on 8 July 2016 was the head of the organization and his wife Bilquis, a nurse, oversees the maternity and adoption services of the foundation. Its headquarters are in Karachi, Pakistan.

"People have become educated, but have yet to become human."
Abdul Sattar Edhi
The Edhi Foundation provides 24-hour emergency assistance across the nation of Pakistan and abroad. The Foundation provides, among many other services, shelter for the destitute, free hospitals and medical care, drug rehabilitation services, and national and international relief efforts. Its main focuses are Emergency Services, Orphans, Handicapped Persons, Shelters, Education, Healthcare, International Community Centers, Blood & Drug Bank, air ambulance services, Marine And Coastal Services.
